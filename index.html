The Lobby

The hotel lobby bar. You know the one — recessed lighting, white marble, those tall chairs nobody sits comfortably in. A TV on mute showing CNN. The bartender polishing glasses that are already clean. It's quiet, it's sterile, it smells like nothing.

Then the crew showed up. One by one, filling the barstools and the couches nearby. The elevator music still playing overhead, but now there's laughter over it. The bartender's actually busy. The lobby that was built for silence got loud — the good kind of loud.

That was Wednesday. By Friday, the party had a new home.
